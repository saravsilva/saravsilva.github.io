short bio

Trained in Biology (FCUL, Portugal, 2003), I joined a PhD Program in Computational Biology (PDBC, Instituto Gulbenkian de Ciência, Portugal), with one year full-time training from international experts. I went on to pursue a PhD thesis in microbial evolutionary genomics, supervised by Eduardo Rocha at Institut Pasteur (Paris, France), defending in September 2010 (Université Pierre et Marie Curie). During this time, I studied bacterial and archaeal ecological adaptation and its genomic signatures, including in human-associated microbial communities. Pursing this line of research, I joined Jeroen Raes lab in Belgium (VIB-KULeuven), where I have been studying gut microbiota metabolic potential and microbial ecological properties such as resilience, with clinical applications in cardiovascular disease and neurogastroenterology.
